**FRAMEWORK**

**Weighing is Essential**: Tell me why your impacts matter more than theirs. Use magnitude, probability, timeframe, and reversibility. Metaweigh if there's competing weighing. "Even if they win X, we still win because Y" is your best friend. If neither side weighs, I'll have to intervene—don't make me do that.

**Argument Extension Standards**: 
- Summary speech must extend everything you want me to evaluate in final focus (yes, including defense)
- Final focus must mirror summary—no new weighing or implications
- Constructive → Rebuttal → Summary → Final Focus. Break this chain and the argument is dropped.

---

**SPEECH-BY-SPEECH**

**Constructive (4 min)**: 
Solid structure matters. I want clear contentions with claim-warrant-impact. Subpoint labels help. Pre-empts are fine but don't sacrifice your case depth.

**Rebuttal (4 min)**:
- **First Rebuttal**: Can be pure offense on the opposing case, but frontlining your own case is strategic
- **Second Rebuttal**: MUST frontline your case. If you don't respond to first rebuttal attacks, I'm buying those responses. "We'll get to that in summary" = you conceded it.

**Summary (3 min)**: 
Collapse the debate. Go for 2-3 strong arguments, not 6 blippy ones. Extend your offense AND your key defensive responses. Provide weighing. This speech should write my ballot. If your final focus partner can't give their speech using only what you said in summary, you messed up.

**Final Focus (2 min)**:
Mirror summary. Crystallize the round to key voting issues with explicit comparison. Tell me: "Judge, you're voting Pro/Con on these two reasons..." No new cards, no new implications, no surprise weighing mechanisms.

---

**EVIDENCE**

I care about evidence quality. Author, year, and source matter. If your evidence is contested in crossfire or speeches:
- Paraphrasing without cards = weak warrant
- Poor source quality (opinion blogs, biased organizations) = I'll discount it
- If you call for evidence and it's miscut or misrepresented, I'll drop the argument

I won't call for cards unless: (1) it's explicitly contested, (2) the round hinges on it, or (3) something seems misrepresented.

---

**CROSSFIRE**

Use it strategically. Get concessions, clarify warrants, expose contradictions. **BUT**: Crossfire isn't speech time. If you get a concession, you MUST mention it in a speech for me to flow it. I'm listening but not flowing crossfire directly.

Grand crossfire can get chaotic—don't all talk over each other. I'll stop flowing if I can't understand anyone.

**THEORY/PROGRESSIVE ARGS**

This is PF, not policy or LD.

- **Framework/Util vs. other FW**: Standard cost-benefit is fine. If you want to run something else, it needs clear warrants and you must win why it's the right evaluative lens. 
- **Kritiks**: This isn't the format for it, but if you insist, it better be well-developed, accessible, and directly relevant to the topic.

**HOW TO WIN MY BALLOT**

2. Weigh your impacts against theirs comparatively
